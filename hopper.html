Hopper

A Bio-Inspired Toy Created in Design Nature

Date

August-October 2016

Class

Design Nature

Through the first year class Design Nature, I learned about the design process and how to take inspiration from existing natural systems. The click beetle, for example, uses an energy storage system that allows it to jump high into the air with a sudden burst of energy. I created several prototypes of my design which closely followed the form of the click beetle. The two leaf-shaped segments folded up as energy was stored in a stretched portion of elastic tubing. A smaller piece of tube wedged around a wooden dowel to create a friction trigger, giving me time to take my hands away before the hopper jumped. “Whiplash” as I named it went through several iterations with the help of Solidworks CAD and rapid prototyping tools such as laser cutters, drill presses, and vertical bandsaws. I also mathematically found a hopping distance estimate based on the energy stored in the stretched tube.

Another main function of this project was to learn how to achieve our own pre-set goals with an audience in mind. For my design goal I decided to focus on members of my high school robotics team. I used my hopper as a tool to demonstrate how simplification can be achieved by studying existing systems.
