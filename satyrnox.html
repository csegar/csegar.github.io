SatyrNox - Synthetic Thespian Robotics

SatyrNox is a new eusocial robotics lab group this semester that focuses on facilitating interactions between humans and robots through animatronic theatrical performances. We started with a first version of an animatronic skeleton made of 3D-printed parts that the software team has been using to work on computer vision and object recognition. As the mechanical lead, I’ve been working with other students to come up with individual or small-group projects that they want to work on to further develop the movements of the creatures while also improving their own design skills. I started the semester doing mostly organizational work getting everyone started on their own projects and setting up a system for working on different sub-assemblies within the main CAD assembly. On my own, I’ve started designing a mouth/jaw movement system that can be integrated into the existing skull design.
